French Onion Soup

We have been trying French onion soup in restaurants for years, and my family and friends agree that none can compare to my recipe for taste and simplicity of preparation.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups sliced onions
  • 4 (10.5 ounce) cans beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons dry sherry (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices French bread
  • 4 slices provolone cheese
  • 2 slices Swiss cheese, diced
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Directions

  1. In an 8-quart stock pot, melt butter with olive oil on medium heat.
  2. Add onions and stir continuously until tender and translucent, without browning them.
  3. Add beef broth, sherry, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Preheat the oven broiler.
  5. Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls, placing one slice of bread on top of each.
  6. Layer each slice of bread with a slice of provolone, diced Swiss cheese, and Parmesan.
  7. Broil until the cheese bubbles and browns slightly. Serve hot.
